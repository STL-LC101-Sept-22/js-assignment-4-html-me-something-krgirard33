Blackberry Chipotle Salsa

I got this recipie off Seantaclaus, who is a great cook who makes amazing food and sometimes writes it down when someone ask how he made something.

Step 1) Mix yourself up a margarita. I am fond of blue margaritas, but I am not a snob and will drink others. And since 2 of the ingredients in the salsa are magarita mix and tequila, it makes it convenient. Maybe make a pitcher of margaritas since I am guessing you are planning on having others over.

Gather up the ingredients, and do the prep work

  • 2/3 bag frozen Blackberrie
  • 1 1/2 cup Raspberries
  • 1 can Pineapple niblets/tidbits/whatever, drained
  • 1 Red Onion minced/chopped small
  • 1 1/2 Red Bell Pepper chopped
  • 8 or 9 cloves minced Garlic
  • 3 Chipotles (it isn't just a resturant)
  • 2 Tbsp Raspberry Balsamic Vinegar
  • 2 shots Tequila
  • 1/3 cup Margarita Mix
  • 2 Tbsp Lemon Juice
  • Various spices if you think it is missing something

What ta do

1) Dump the blackberries, raspberries, red onion, 1/2 the can of pineapple, garlic and 3 chipotles into the food processor, and run it until everything is shredded/minced.

2) Pour the mixture into a saucepan over medium heat to try and condense the liquid a bit, eventually adding the rest of the ingredients in the listed order. Yes, even the remaining 1/2 can of pineapple. This is why you got the tiny chunks version.

3) Sample it at this point, see if you need to add any spices, like maybe some powdered Ancho.

4) Place in the fridge till it is no longer warm.

Now place it in front of people, say "Bon appétit!", and take a drink of your margarita as you channel your inner Julia Child.
