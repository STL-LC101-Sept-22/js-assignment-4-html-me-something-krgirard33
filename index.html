Drunk and Clueless in the Kitchen

Adventures in cooking & life

Hello, and welcome to Drunk and Clueless in the Kitchen!

I am not a good cook, but I like to do it. And, like Julia Childs, I feel that having some alcohol while cooking makes one more creative.

My biggest issue in cooking is all the prep... I am slow and careful becuase I have no idea what I am doing, so they say 10 min prep, for me it is more like 20.

I am also bad at judging portions. If I try to cook for 2 I'll make enough for 5. I blame this on learning to cook as a bottomless pit teenager who then became a grass cutter who was eating 3500+ cals a day and not gaining weight.

I often just see a recipie and say "Hey, that looks good". For example I saw a picture of ratatouille, said that looks simple, hit up Amazon, ordered a mandoline (Once I spelled it correctly and quit getting offered a tiny guitar), and 3 short weeks later I spent way too long slicing veggies up. It came out ok, and I said I am never making that agin.

Another time I saw something called Hasselback Butternut Squash. No idea what it was, so I had to look it up and make it. Butternut squash, maple syrup, pecans, & pumpkin spice all roasted in the oven. Hasselback is apparently just cutting 95% of the way through something so that you have all these little slices about 1/8th of an inch apart, which lets you get all the other ingredients down in there. I figured out it helps if you put something like a rolling pin under the section you are working on so it fans out the slices.

The kids still talk about the time I made grey eggs. I make what I call concrete. A ton of things that would be good in an omlet (ham, green peppers, mushrooms, onions, baccon bits, etc), with enough scrambled eggs to make it all into a solid mass once cooked. This time I decided to add a little worcestershire sauce to the eggs. The kids took one look at the grey stuff I put on their plates and decided they were not hungry. It tasted great.

Sometimes the stuff I make is more "What's in the fridge?", like some Napa cabbage, ground beef, carrots, parsnips, garlic, some soy & sriracha sauce, for a fast and easy meal.
